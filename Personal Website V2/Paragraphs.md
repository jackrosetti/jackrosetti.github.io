Fan Paragraph
When I first dove into philosophy, I had difficulty reading and understanding the significance of classical texts. Was Plato a fascist? Did Descartes <i>really</i> think there was an evil demon? Turning to academic texts proved futile for a novice as they were, frankly, boring and laden with impenetrable academic jargon. The more I progressed in my philosophy career, however, the easier it eventually became to understand and critically engage with the text. With Făn, I wanted to create a completely free online magazine whose mission is to disseminate critiques, explications, and elaborations. In sum: philosophy. 

Friends and I run Făn democratically. We strive to discuss philosophy in an engaging, interesting way that still retains rigorous analysis. Too often, we see pop-philosophy dominate the public sphere. The prominence of egregious self-help books which are, functionally, regurgitations of stoic and/or existentialist philosophy only confirms this. We would never claim to be able to answer all questions in philosophy; sometimes we cannot even answer <i>one</i> question. Rather, we wish to display the vast wealth of food for thought. At the end of the day, we consider it a victory if we widen the lens through which an individual views life.

My hope is that this publication establishes itself as a free publication of rigor. We also plan on branching out into print media. 



INTERESTS
I am mainly interested in the economics of inequality and income inequality. For this reason, I'm drawn to thinkers like Thomas Piketty and Joseph Stiglitz. I think that for too long the economy has not been organized to benefit the average worker. For that reason, I plan on working in public policy or some similar field; I want to better the lives of the common worker. 

In terms of philosophy, I am mainly drawn to phenomenology, deconstruction, and the philosophy of language. Derrida, Heidegger, Quine and Wittgenstein have influenced my thinking the most to this point.
