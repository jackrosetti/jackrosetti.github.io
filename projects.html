Fan Publication

A non-partisan philosophy magazine delivered monthly.

When I first dove into philosophy, I had difficulty reading and understanding the significance of classical texts. Was Plato a fascist? Did Descartes really think there was an evil demon? Turning to academic texts proved futile for a novice as they were, frankly, boring and laden with impenetrable academic jargon. The more I progressed in my philosophy career, however, the easier it eventually became to understand and critically engage with the text. With Făn, I wanted to create a completely free online magazine whose mission is to disseminate critiques, explications, and elaborations. In sum: philosophy.

Friends and I run Făn democratically. We strive to discuss philosophy in an engaging, interesting way that still retains rigorous analysis. Too often, we see pop-philosophy dominate the public sphere. The prominence of egregious self-help books which are, functionally, regurgitations of stoic and/or existentialist philosophy only confirms this. We would never claim to be able to answer all questions in philosophy; sometimes we cannot even answer one question. Rather, we wish to display the vast wealth of food for thought. At the end of the day, we consider it a victory if we widen the lens through which an individual views life.

My hope is that this publication establishes itself as a free publication of rigor. We also plan on branching out into print media.

Rosetti Terminal

A knockoff Bloomberg Terminal for stock manipulation and analysis.

I have always been interested in the intersection of Computer Science and Economics. That's why when I found Stocker, I knew I was in for a treat. Stocker is an open-source Python library that uses a few different libraries to manipulate stock information. This piqued my interested, so I decided to write a front-end for it. I had a blast doing so, learning a lot about web scraping and data parsing.

The program makes sure the user finds the correct company out of the thousands that are publicly available for trade. It scrapes the NASDAQ website and returns results similar to what the user prompts (e.g., if the user chooses "Apple" but really mean "Apple Inc."). Then, Stocker creates a stock object with different functions available to manipulate and get the data of that stock.

This project was a joyride for me as a novice developer. I am very grateful to have found the library. In the future, I hope to create a free web application out of this. I think that, too often, people are deprived of the necessary tools for investing. It creates an uneven playing field. If this product exists for free, it can reduce the gap in available tools. Milky Way Visualizer

Watch the planets go by and by.

Isn't the solar system interesting? Isn't college scary? I first sat down to make this project a week before I began my undergraduate career. I wanted to refresh my Python skills in preparation for what was to come. So, what better way to accomplish that than by creating a mathematically accurate model of the solar system and its movements?

I had to do a lot of research on how planets even move. There are, unsurprisingly, a lot of factors which influence the movement of a planet within a certain period of time. Researching and getting the necessary math down took a while, but after that things sort of fell into place. The numerous libraries in Python facilitated this project -- especially the wonderful math libraries.

Projectile Simulator

Launch a ball at different speeds and angles.

Shortly following my AP Computer Science A class, I started to work more with Java graphics -- something I both love and detest. This project was also an exercise of my ability to understand physics, as I have never been taught each facet of a motion equation.

My goal for this project was to design a nice, simple interface that does not overload users. In my research, I found a LOT of poorly designed interfaces (you could tell they were made by engineers). I believe there is some beauty in the simplicity of my desgin: three buttons, two sliders, and one purpose: launch a ball.

It seldom happens, but I am not sure that I have future plans for this project. I think this could potentially be one part of a larger physics visualizer, but that would be a long way's away.
