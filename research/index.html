Labor Market Conditions and Military Enlistment

Senior Thesis — UCSD Department of Economics

This paper addresses how local labor markets affect military enlistment across states. Prior studies focused on national unemployment and enlistment, disregarding geographical heterogeneity. I develop a cohort-based identification strategy to examine how regional labor market shocks drive enlistment decisions.

Estimating the Black-White Parity in Returns to Education

Honors Paper — UCSD Department of Economics

This paper measures the Black-White disparity in returns to education using an extended Mincer equation. Workers are grouped by commuting zone to capture both metropolitan and agricultural local economies. Nonparametric estimation techniques uncover demographic differences in returns to education.

Fuzzy Regression Discontinuity with a Continuous Treatment Effect

With Haitian Xie

Standard fuzzy RD designs use a binary treatment to identify causal effects. This project extends the framework to continuous treatment variables and applies it to analyze how distance from time zone borders affects time use behavior.

Income Tax Analysis Using County-Level Data

With Dr. Christy Caridi

This project studies wealth disparities using IRS county-level income tax data. Results show richer counties pay substantially more in capital gains taxes and experienced faster income growth relative to lower-income counties.
